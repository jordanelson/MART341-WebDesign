Glacier National Park

Glacier National Park is 1,583 square miles of wilderness situated in Montana's Rocky Mountains. It's glacier-carved peaks and and valleys run to the Canadian border. There are over 700 miles of hiking trails among pristine forests, alpine meadows, rugged mountains, and spectacular lakes. Visitors have the oppurtunity to relive past times through historic chalets, lodges, and the famous Going-to-the-Sun Road. The raw beauty of GNP is best accessable in the summer months, but it can still be enjoyed during the winter too.
